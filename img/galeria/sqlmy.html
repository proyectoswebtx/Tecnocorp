MySQL Logo

database management system
Updated: Aug 27, 2020

MySQL is a software, developed by Oracle Corporation in 1995. The software helps in managing databases and is used in many applications and social networks.

Meaning and history

The MySQL logo consists of a wordmark with an emblem on its left. It is pretty simple and laconic, yet well-recognizable across the globe.

The MySQL name’s inscription is executed in a modern sans-serif typeface, where the letters are slightly extended and have smooth sleek liked with rounded angles.

The “My” is colored blue, while the “SQL” is orange. The company’s name was formed from the founder’s daughter’s name (My) and an abbreviation of Structured Query Language.

The emblem of the company, a dolphin silhouette, is placed at the end of the wordmark, and moves up and left. The kind and playful animal is a symbol of friendship, support, and protection. Dolphin also represents balance and harmony.

The blue and orange color combination of the MySQL logo is a representation of the stable and reputable company, which values security and quality of services. It also reflects the authority and professionalism of the brand, evoking a sense of reliability and confidence.
